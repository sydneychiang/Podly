Assessing Personal Risk

With data collected from Texas Medical Association

Low Risk

Opening the mail, Getting restaurant takeout, Pumping Gasoline, Playing Tennis, Going Camping

Low-Moderate Risk

Grocery Shopping, Going for a walk/run/bike ride with others, Playing golf, Staying at a hotel for two nights, Sitting in a doctor's waiting room, Going to a library or museum, Outdoor dinings, Walking in a busy downtown, Spending an hour at a playground

Moderate Risk

Having dinner at someone else's house, Attending a backyard barbecue, Going to the beach, Shopping at a mall, Sending kids to school/camp/day care, Working a week in an office building, Swimming in a public pool, Visiting an elderly relative or friend in their home

Moderate-High Risk

Going to a hair salon or barbershop, Indoor dining, Attending a wedding or funeral, Traveling by plane, Playing basketball, Playing football, Hugging or shaking hands when greeting a friend

High Risk

Eating at a buffet, Working out at a gym, Going to an amusement park, Going to a movie theater, Attending a large music concert, Going to a sports stadium, Attending a religious service with 500+ worshipers, Going to a bar
